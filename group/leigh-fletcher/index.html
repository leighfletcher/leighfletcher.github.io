Biography

Dr. Leigh Fletcher is a Planetary Scientist specialising in the exploration of our Solar System’s giant planets via robotic spacecraft missions and ground-based astronomical facilities. He is a Royal Society University Research Fellow (URF) and Associate Professor in Planetary Sciences at the University of Leicester. He is a co-investigator on the Cassini mission to Saturn, the JUICE mission to Jupiter, and a passionate advocate for future exploration of the distance Ice Giants. He currently leads a planetary atmospheres team at the University of Leicester, funded by the Royal Society, STFC, and the European Research Council.
